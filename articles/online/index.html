Online CS 61A

This page describes the online format of CS 61A. For general information about the course, see the syllabus.

Students can officially enroll in CS 61A and take the course fully online. It is possible to take the course remotely from anywhere in the world. It is also possible for students enrolled in the on-campus course who need to travel away from Berkeley for part of term to continue participating using the online resources. Online offerings of CS 61A provide the same amount of course credit and fulfill all of the same requirements as the on-campus course.

Online vs On-Campus

All core aspects of the on-campus course are replicated in the online version of CS 61A, including lectures, labs, discussions, and office hours. All students use the same online discussion forum to ask questions and receive announcements. Optional small-group and one-on-one tutoring that is sometimes offered via student groups (CS Mentors) or pedagogy courses (CS 370) will not be available for online students.

Our experience indicates that most students do benefit from the on-campus experience because in-person labs, discussions, and office hours are more conducive to participation and connecting with other students. So, if you have a choice between on-campus or online formats, choose on-campus. You can still use the online resources freely as you prefer.

Enrolling in Online CS 61A

For Summer 2019, remote students should enroll in CS W61A, which fulfills all of the same requirements as CS 61A. Despite the fact that these two courses list different instructors, they will be taught together by the same instructor team. Tiffany Perumpail, Christopher Allsman, and Alex Stennet are the primary instructors, while John DeNero will support the course and produce lecture video content that will be available to all students enrolled in both courses. Students enrolled in W61A will be assigned to online lab and discussion, while CS 61A students will be assigned to on-campus lab and discussion.

Online lab and discussion sections meet at a particular time and are hosted by the course staff in the same way as on-campus sections. See the course calendar for section times and connection instructions. Students are still expected to attend and participate, just like in an on-campus section.

Auditing Online CS 61A

All materials for the course are posted to cs61a.org and are freely available to remote students from around the world. You're welcome to work through them at your own pace (audit the course), but no course credit or support is available to students who are not officially enrolled.

Exams

Online exams will be held at the same time as on-campus exams when possible. Exams are typically proctored via video conference, but in some cases an external live proctor can be arranged (for example if a group of students at a remote university are taking the course together). Software

We use two tools to coordinate online sections, Slack and Zoom. Slack is a group chat application that runs all the time, so that students in online sections can coordinate with each other and the staff. Zoom is a video conferencing platform that is only available during section times.

Slack. If you are enrolled in an online section or wish to attend an online section even though you're enrolled in a regular section, join the online Slack by requesting an invitation here (only available when courses are in session):

slackin.app.cs61a.org

All online-specific coordination and announcements will be made through Slack. Check it! Ask your questions there for quick responses.

Once you join Slack, you should join the channel for your section. That's where section-specific content will be posted.

Zoom. When you first join a Zoom meeting for discussion, you will be asked to download their software. You can also join from a mobile device, but using your computer is better because you can share your screen with staff and other students. Read the Zoom student guide. If you get stuck or don't know how to do something, there's a #zoom channel on Slack to ask questions.

Handouts

Discussion handouts are normally distributed in on-campus discussion sections. These are also posted to cs61a.org, and you can download them there. We recommend that you print out the handout in advance of each online discussion section.

Lab does not have any printed material. Students use the same lab materials in online and on-campus lab sections.

Getting Help

Online office hours will also be coordinated through Slack, and you will have opportunities to talk one-on-one with course staff members as needed.

If you are enrolled in an online section but are able to come to campus, you are still welcome to come to on-campus office hours, homework parties, project parties, and review sessions.

If you find that online sections don't work well for you, please contact one of the course instructor for guidance, such as John DeNero (denero@berkeley.edu).
